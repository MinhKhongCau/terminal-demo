ALL ABOARD

Born in a forgotten freight house on the edge of the old line, The Terminal was built from salvaged oak, wrought iron, and the kind of patience the railways used to demand. Step through our doors and the city's hum gives way to the soft hiss of espresso and the slow turn of brass cogs.

Our kitchen serves the dishes our great-grandparents carried across borders — Vienna's schnitzel, Budapest's goulash, Lyon's confit — plated as if for the dining car of the Orient Express. Every plate is a postcard. Every coffee, a ticket.
